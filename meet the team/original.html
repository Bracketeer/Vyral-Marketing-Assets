Dear fellow business owner,

Are you staying in touch with your database like you should be?

The #1 most common mistake business owners make is not communicating with their customers, past clients, unconverted leads, and the people they’ve met regularly.

These people want to hear from you - they need your on-going expertise.

But if you don’t stay in touch in a valuable way they will hire your competition and you’ll miss out on referrals and repeat sales.

You’re also leaving money on the table with your unconverted leads.

It’s a common problem. It’s easy to get caught up in talking to strangers or buying attention for new business.

You forget it makes sense to talk to people who know, like and trust you first.

Think about all the people you know (who fit your target customer profile or could refer you to them) on Facebook, LinkedIn, in your mobile device, your email account, CRM, or lead generation program(s)).

All those people need regular, systematic communication from you!

Your database is your #1 business asset – it sends you referral and repeat business that’s far more profitable and enjoyable than talking to people who don’t know you.

Working your database is the least expensive way to grow your business.

Your customer and prospect database is your wealth (investors pay for your people, process, and database) and allows you to exit your business someday if you want.

So how can you do a better job of staying in touch with your database?
